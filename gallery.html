DI and SunLine Transit Agency Joint Press Release


Diversified Industries C&IS, based in Orlando, Florida, was awarded the Implementation Contract for System Maintenance Automated Repair and Test (SMART) software by SunLine Transit Agency, June 2017. SunLine Transit Agency is a Southern California public transportation agency that is based in Thousand Palms, California. SMART Software integration, testing and end-user training was successfully completed November 2017. - Official Joint Press Release -



DI awarded service agreement from SunLine Transit Agency


DI at the SunLine Facility in Thousand Palms, California

SunLine Transit Agency has been leading the front on alternative fuel buses in the US and is on a clear path to an all zero emission fleet. SMART was implemented for use on SunLine's hydrogen battery electric-zero emission bus (ZEB). Ten (10) are currently in service with ten (10) additional ZEBs being delivered to Sunline starting early 2019.



DI invited to view the first unmanned launch of Orion


DI and others at the first launch of the Orion spacecraft.

The Orion spacecraft is NASAs newest capsule intended to launch astronauts to the Moon, Mars, and beyond. Exploration Flight Test 1 launched the Orion capsule on top of a Delta IV Heavy from Launch Complex 37B at Cape Canaveral.

65th Annual National Business Aviation Association Conference


Astronaut Julie Payette and Martin Belson at NBAA Canadian sponsored luncheon

President Martin Belson attended the National Business Aviation Association's (NBAA) 65th annual Meeting and Conference in Orlando Florida at the Orange County Convention Center. He also attended a private networking luncheon with 50+ Canadian companies sponsored by the Consulate General of Canada Ms. Louise Louis Leger. Also in attendance was the Canadian Trade Commissioner Ms. Sandra Peart, and Canadian Astronaut Ms. Julie Payette who has flown on Space Shuttle Discovery, Endeavor and on the International Space Station. Ms. Payette was also a keynote speaker at the luncheon. The meeting led to other very productive follow-up meetings with the Trade Commissioner.



Meeting with NASA's Chief Technologist


Martin Belson with Dr. Mason Peck

DI was one of three speakers invited to speak at the Kennedy Space Center (KSC) Space Life Science Laboratory (SLSL) to the NASA Chief Technologist, Dr. Mason Peck. Dr. Peck was visiting KSC from NASA headquarters in Washington D.C.. Mr. Martin Belson was asked to tell the story of DI's commercialization effort of the SMART software. Following their presentations, the DI team met with Dr. Peck and the NASA Innovative Partnerships Chief Officer Dave Makufka. Dr. Peck suggested to Mr. Makufka that his staff at KSC work with DI to spin-back SMART technology into other NASA programs. The NASA/KSC Innovative Partnership Office has indicated via e-mail that they would like to host a meeting with DI to discuss infusion opportunities for SMART.
